# ScheduleLab — Project Brief

Running a small equipment hire operation sounds deceptively simple: someone needs a crane, you have a crane, money changes hands. In practice, of course, it's a beautiful disaster of phone calls, whiteboard scrawls, lost paperwork, and operators asking "wait, which site am I going to again?" ScheduleLab was built to fix that. It's a web-based scheduling and job management platform that takes operators from the first customer enquiry all the way through to a signed, submitted site docket — without a single Post-it note in sight. Customers submit enquiries through a simple public form, dispatchers turn those into jobs, allocate the right gear and personnel, and the whole thing lands on a calendar that actually makes sense.

Out on site, the platform earns its keep. Operators open their job on any mobile device, work through a digital site docket — safety checklists, hazard logs, equipment hours, photos, the lot — and sign off with a captured signature before submitting. Back at the office, dispatchers see completed dockets in real time, assets are tracked against compliance expiries (no more "oops, the CraneSafe lapsed"), and customer records hold a clean history of every job ever run. There's even a projects layer for long-running engagements, so recurring work doesn't have to be rebuilt from scratch every fortnight.

ScheduleLab is squarely aimed at smaller operators — the kinds of businesses that have outgrown the whiteboard but aren't ready (or willing) to wrestle with the enterprise behemoths that cost more per month than a machine's insurance premium. It's deliberately straightforward: three roles (Admin, Dispatcher, Operator), one coherent workflow, and no feature bloat from use cases that don't apply to a tight-knit crew running a handful of assets. Big enough to professionalise the operation; small enough that everyone will actually use it.
